Beta-Lactams

Activity: AUC The absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion (ADME) of beta-lactam antibiotics play a crucial role in their pharmacokinetic profile. Following oral administration, beta-lactams are generally well-absorbed from the gastrointestinal tract, although absorption rates can vary among different agents. Distribution occurs widely throughout the body, with beta-lactams reaching therapeutic concentrations in various tissues and body fluids, including the cerebrospinal fluid. Metabolism of beta-lactams is minimal, with the majority of the drugs excreted unchanged or as inactive metabolites primarily through renal filtration. Adjustments in dosing are often necessary in patients with impaired renal function to prevent drug accumulation and potential toxicity. Understanding the ADME properties of beta-lactams is crucial for optimizing their therapeutic efficacy and minimizing adverse effects.
